About Me

I'm Naman Shankar, a recent graduate from IIT Kharagpur, with a strong foundation in designing and deploying AI-powered solutions across diverse domains. My expertise lies in computer vision, natural language processing (NLP), data analysis, and automation systems — with a focus on building tools that deliver measurable real-world impact.

Over the past few years, I've worked extensively on projects involving biomedical image processing, vehicle and license plate recognition, ad and spam detection in emails, and intelligent content categorization. From using OpenCV and YOLO for vision tasks to implementing Streamlit dashboards, BeautifulSoup scrapers, and NLP pipelines, I bring an end-to-end development approach to my work.

During my time at KGP, I actively contributed to the institute's technical and leadership culture. I served as the Vice President of the Civil Engineering Society and was part of the organizing team for Megalith, our annual departmental fest — both of which gave me opportunities to lead, collaborate, and think beyond code.

I'm particularly passionate about applying AI in meaningful contexts — be it in healthcare, mobility, or digital communication — and am always keen to work on challenges where technology meets impact.
