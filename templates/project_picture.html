What's In The Picture

January 2024 - March 2024 Carselona Daily

Project Overview

The "What's In The Picture" project involved developing an automated system for analyzing car service photos. The system was designed to perform three key functions: verify image authenticity to detect manipulated images, analyze vehicle door status (open/closed), and extract information from license plates. This solution aimed to enhance the efficiency and reliability of automotive service documentation.

Technologies Used

VGG-19 YOLOv8 KerasOCR Python TensorFlow Image Processing Computer Vision

Key Features

  • Image Authenticity Verification

    Developed a robust image classification system leveraging VGG-19 to distinguish between genuine and manipulated images, helping prevent fraud in service documentation.

  • Vehicle Door Status Detection

    Implemented YOLOv8 for precise car detection and door status analysis, enabling automated verification of vehicle condition during service inspections.

  • License Plate Recognition

    Integrated PaddleOCR for accurate number plate recognition, allowing for automated vehicle identification and record-keeping.

  • Comprehensive Analysis Pipeline

    Created a unified analysis workflow that processes images through multiple detection models while maintaining high accuracy across diverse lighting conditions and camera angles.

Results and Impact

The project delivered an overall accuracy of 88% across its various detection and recognition components. By automating the analysis of service photos, the system significantly reduced the time required for manual verification while enhancing the reliability of vehicle documentation. This technology helps automotive service providers streamline their operations, reduce fraud, and improve customer trust through accurate record-keeping.
